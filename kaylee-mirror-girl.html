The Disappearance of Kaylee M. – The “Mirror Girl” of OSU

By Staff Writer | October 16, 2017

On the night of October 14, 2017, Ohio State University student Kaylee M., 21, left a house party on East 14th Avenue in Columbus a little after 2:00 a.m. Friends described her as “buzzed but cheerful,” carrying a half-finished drink and joking that she “needed to fix her eyeliner” before bed.

Her roommate later told police she heard Kaylee in the bathroom, talking softly to herself and occasionally laughing. At first, it seemed like nothing unusual—until the sounds stopped abruptly.

The bathroom door was unlocked. Inside, the light was still on, and a hairbrush lay on the floor. The mirror over the sink was unusually cold to the touch despite the apartment’s heating being on full blast. Kaylee was gone.

According to the official missing person’s report, the last known activity on her phone was a Snapchat video sent to a friend at 2:37 a.m. The video showed only her reflection in the bathroom mirror. Halfway through, the reflection blinked out of sync with her—once, slowly—and smiled before the video cut to static.

Kaylee’s body has never been found. To this day, students at OSU still whisper about the “Mirror Girl,” warning others not to linger too long in front of a bathroom mirror when intoxicated. Some believe she’s still there—waiting to trade places.
