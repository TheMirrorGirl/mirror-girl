The Disappearance of Kaylee M. – The “Mirror Girl” of OSU

By Staff Writer | October 16, 2017

On the night of October 14, 2017, Ohio State University student Kaylee M., aged 21, left a house party on East 14th Avenue in Columbus shortly after 2:00 a.m. Witnesses described her as “buzzed but cheerful,” holding a half-finished drink and joking she “needed to fix her eyeliner” before going to bed.

Kaylee’s roommate later told police she heard Kaylee speaking softly to herself in the bathroom and laughing intermittently. At first, the sounds seemed harmless — until they stopped abruptly without explanation.

The bathroom door was unlocked when checked. Inside, the light remained on, and a hairbrush was lying on the floor. The mirror above the sink was reported as unusually cold, despite the apartment’s heating system running full blast. Kaylee was nowhere to be found.

According to official missing persons reports, the last activity on Kaylee’s phone was a Snapchat video sent at 2:37 a.m. to a friend. The video captured only her reflection in the bathroom mirror. Partway through, her reflection blinked out of sync — once, deliberately — and smiled before the footage abruptly cut to static.

Kaylee’s body has never been recovered. Her disappearance has since sparked rumors among OSU students about the “Mirror Girl,” a ghostly figure said to haunt the campus. Students warn that if you’re intoxicated and stare into a mirror for too long, you may see Kaylee’s reflection smiling back — and risk swapping places.
